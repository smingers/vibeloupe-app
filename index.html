The speed trap: Your team is shipping more than ever — features go out weekly, the backlog never empties. But six months from now, will you be able to say you learned something definitive about your users?
Vibeloupe brings the Build-Measure-Learn loop directly into your IDE. Every hypothesis tested. Every sprint connected to a learning goal. Every plan pressure-tested before a single line of code gets written.

The Toolkit

Four skills.
One closed loop.

01
/hypothesis-validator

Test Before You Build

Turn a raw idea or hunch into a falsifiable experiment — with a ranked list of assumptions and the cheapest test that could kill it.

  • Risk-ranked assumptions so you know exactly what to test first
  • Tiered experiments from 1-day customer calls to functional MVPs
  • Built-in interview coaching so your conversations produce signal, not reassurance
02
/spec-review

The Feedback Your Team Won't Give You

Paste any plan, PRD, or strategy doc and get sharp, opinionated analysis through 8 analytical lenses — in minutes, not a week of review cycles.

  • Catches the hidden assumption your whole plan rests on
  • Flags motivated reasoning before you're too invested to pivot
  • 3 specific recommendations you can act on this week
03
/learn-loop

Weekly Sprints That Build Knowledge

Run a structured Build-Measure-Learn session every week. Plan what you're testing, then debrief what you actually learned — not what you hoped to learn.

  • Forces falsifiable hypotheses, not vague "learning goals"
  • Captures the one belief that, if wrong, makes everything else moot
  • Tracks carry / pivot / abandon decisions across every week
04
/sprint-recap

Connect Your Commits to Your Learning

See whether last week's code actually tested what you were trying to learn — or if you shipped a bunch of stuff with no connection to your open experiments.

  • Classifies commits into user-facing vs. internal work
  • Flags experiments that have gone untouched for 2+ weeks
  • Surfaces drift before it turns into a wasted quarter

How it works

A closed loop
that compounds.

The four skills aren't standalone tools — they're a connected system. Every experiment gets logged. Every sprint gets cross-referenced. Every plan review feeds next week's learning goals. And it all lives in your repo.

  • 1

    Hypothesize

    Crystallize ideas into testable experiments with explicit pass/fail criteria.

  • 2

    Build & Measure

    Ship the minimum experiment. Capture what you observe, not what you interpret.

  • 3

    Learn & Decide

    Debrief weekly. Carry, pivot, or abandon. Update beliefs with real evidence.

  • 4

    Compound Over Time

    Your experiment log travels with the codebase. Knowledge doesn't walk out the door.
